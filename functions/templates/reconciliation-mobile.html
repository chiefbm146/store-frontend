Understanding

What is Reconciliation?

Reconciliation is about establishing and maintaining a mutually respectful relationship between Indigenous and non-Indigenous peoples. For this to happen, there has to be awareness of the past, acknowledgement of the harm that has been inflicted, atonement for the causes, and action to change behavior.

The Truth and Reconciliation Commission (TRC) was created to document the truth of survivors, families, communities, and anyone personally affected by the residential school experience. Through this process, we are called to learn about our shared history and commit to building a better future together.

Moon Tide Reconciliation is committed to being part of this journey, offering education, cultural experiences, and pathways to understanding that honor Indigenous wisdom and traditions.

TRC Legacy

The 94 Calls to Action

The Truth and Reconciliation Commission released 94 Calls to Action in 2015, outlining concrete steps toward reconciliation. These calls address child welfare, education, language and culture, health, and justice.

1-42

Legacy

Addressing child welfare, education, language and culture, health, and justice reform to heal the wounds of residential schools and create lasting change.

43-76

Reconciliation

Actions for government, churches, commemoration of residential school history, missing children, and education for reconciliation across all sectors.

77-94

Professional Development

Training and cultural competency development for public servants, health professionals, journalists, lawyers, and all those working with Indigenous peoples.
